## Purpose
In this task, you will build a clear understanding of what data analysis is, how it works using the IPO model, and how it helps people make better decisions in real life.

## How This Assignment Fits The Lesson
This assignment comes after the Lesson 00 lesson plan and companion tutorial.
Use the lesson and tutorial ideas first, then complete the assignment to show your understanding.

There is no Jupyter notebook in Lesson 00.
That is intentional.
This lesson is about building the big-picture understanding that students will need before they begin notebook work in later lessons.

## Part 1: Explain It Simply (Process Checkpoint)
In your own words, explain:
- What is data analysis?
- Why do people use data instead of guessing?

Keep it simple, as if you are explaining it to someone who has never taken this course.

Optional thinking evidence (any format):
- bullet notes
- quick brainstorm
- voice note
- sketch or diagram

## Part 2: IPO Sorting and Thinking
Using class examples (or your tutorial notes), sort tasks into:
- Input
- Process
- Output

Then add one of your own examples for each category.

After sorting, answer:
- Why is it important that these steps happen in the correct order?

## Part 3: Real-World Scenario (Choice and Context)
Choose one scenario, or create your own:
- a school decision (for example, cafeteria food choices or exam scheduling)
- a community issue (for example, cooling centres, transit, or recycling)
- a personal decision (for example, choosing a phone plan or tracking fitness)

Your task:
1. Describe the decision.
2. Identify:
   - Input (What data would you collect?)
   - Process (What would you do to the data?)
   - Output (What would you produce?)
3. Explain:
   - How does the data help make a better decision?
   - What could go wrong if the data is messy or incomplete?

## Part 4: Quick Explain (Conversation Check)
Be ready to explain (1-2 minutes):
- one part of your IPO example
- why it matters for decision-making

This can be done with a partner or the teacher.

## Final Product (Your Choice)
Show your thinking in a format that works for you:
- short written response
- slide deck
- infographic
- short video or audio explanation
- annotated diagram

## Success Criteria (Aligned to Expectations)
You are successful when you:

### Understanding and Thinking (A1.1, A3.1)
- clearly explain data analysis in simple language
- accurately use the IPO model to describe a workflow
- show logical reasoning about how data supports decisions

### Application (A1.2)
- correctly classify tasks as Input, Process, or Output
- apply IPO to a real-world or personal scenario

### Connections (A2.1)
- explain how data impacts real decisions (personal, local, or global)
- consider what happens when data is incomplete or misleading

## Teacher Notes (PACT-Based Rationale)
PACT is used to design assignments that promote real learning in an AI-rich classroom by making thinking visible, personal, and meaningful.

### P - Process, Not Product
- This assignment emphasizes reasoning steps, not just a final answer.
- Students explain how they classified tasks and why IPO sequence matters.
- Checkpoints (Part 1, IPO sort, quick explain) make learning process visible.

### A - Agency and Student Voice
- Students choose a scenario that matters to them.
- Students choose a final format (written, slides, infographic, audio/video, diagram).
- Choice supports ownership and authentic student voice.

### C - Context and Lived Experience
- Students apply IPO to school, community, or personal decisions.
- Prompts prioritize local and lived contexts over generic responses.
- Real-world relevance strengthens meaning and transfer.

### T - Transparent AI Use
- AI use is allowed for support (for example brainstorming or re-explaining).
- AI replacement of core thinking is restricted.
- Students must disclose tool, prompt, edits, and learning impact.

### Big Idea
PACT helps design work that is harder to fake and more meaningful to complete.
