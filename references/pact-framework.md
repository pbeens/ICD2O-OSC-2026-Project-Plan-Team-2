# PACT Framework for AI-Rich Assignment Design

PACT is a framework for designing assignments that promote real learning in an AI-rich world. It focuses on making learning more human, visible, and meaningful.

## P - Process, not Product
Focus on how students think, not just what they submit.
- Emphasize planning, drafts, checkpoints, and decision-making.
- Ask students to explain why they made choices.
- Collect process evidence, not only final products.

## A - Agency and Student Voice
Focus on student choice and ownership.
- Allow choice of topic, format, audience, or approach where possible.
- Preserve space for student perspective and voice.
- Use options that are harder to outsource and easier to personalize.

## C - Context and Lived Experience
Focus on real-world and local relevance.
- Connect tasks to classroom learning, school/community issues, or personal contexts.
- Use authentic prompts grounded in lived experience.
- Require context-aware reasoning, not generic responses.

## T - Transparent AI Use
Focus on clear, honest, and responsible AI use.
- State what AI use is allowed and what is restricted.
- Require students to document AI use (tool, prompt, edits, learning impact).
- Position AI as a support tool, not a replacement for student thinking.

## Big Idea
PACT designs assignments where thinking is visible, answers are personal, context matters, and AI supports learning instead of replacing it.

## In One Sentence
PACT makes assignments harder to fake and more meaningful to complete.
